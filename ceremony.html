Anand Karaj

The heart of the Sikh teachings is Oneness; the existence of single Universal, Creative Force that nourishes humanity. A Sikh's responsibility is to be a wellspring of compassion in recognition of this Oneness. Regardless of where she steps, a Sikh who reads, learns, and practices the Sikh teachings spreads love in all four directions, and empowers those in need.

The Anand Karaj is a time where two individuals walk the Sikh path of Universal Love together, as equal partners, regardless of their race, religion, gender, class, or caste. Written by the fourth Sikh master, Guru Ram Das Ji, in Panjab in the 16th Century, the Anand Karaj is composed in four poetic stanzas to musical measure and describes the joyful union of the two hearts with the Great Mother of all creation, whose radiance exists in all.
